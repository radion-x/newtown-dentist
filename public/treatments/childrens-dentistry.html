Children's Dentistry

Gentle, friendly dental care to give your children a positive start and set them up for a lifetime of healthy smiles.

Making Dental Visits Fun

At Dental on King, we believe that positive early experiences at the dentist are crucial for lifelong oral health. Our friendly team takes a gentle, patient approach that helps children feel comfortable and even excited about caring for their teeth.

We understand that every child is different. Some may be nervous about their first visit, while others are curious and eager. We take the time to explain everything in child-friendly terms, let them explore our equipment, and make each visit a positive experience.

From first tooth checkups to fluoride treatments and fissure sealants, we provide comprehensive dental care tailored to your child's developing needs.

Tips for Parents

1

Start Early

Begin dental visits around age 1 or when the first tooth appears. Early visits establish a dental home and catch problems early.

2

Keep It Positive

Avoid using dentist visits as threats. Talk about dental care positively to help your child feel comfortable.

3

Brush Together

Children learn by example. Brush your teeth alongside them to make it a fun family activity.

4

Limit Sugary Snacks

Reduce frequency of sugary foods and drinks. Water and healthy snacks help protect teeth.

Frequently Asked Questions

When should my child first visit the dentist?

We recommend a first visit around age 1 or within 6 months of the first tooth appearing. This helps establish good habits early and allows us to monitor development.

How often should children have checkups?

Most children benefit from checkups every 6 months. This allows us to catch any issues early and reinforce good oral hygiene habits.

What are fissure sealants?

Sealants are thin protective coatings applied to the chewing surfaces of back teeth. They fill in the grooves where bacteria and food particles can get trapped, helping prevent decay.

My child is nervous about the dentist. What can I do?

This is very common! We're experienced in helping anxious children feel comfortable. Visit us beforehand, read positive books about dentists, and let us take it at their pace.
