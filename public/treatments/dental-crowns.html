Dental Crowns

Restore damaged teeth to their natural strength and beauty with high-quality porcelain crowns.

Protect and Restore Your Teeth

A dental crown is a custom-made cap that covers and protects a damaged or weakened tooth, restoring its shape, size, strength, and appearance. Crowns are one of the most versatile solutions in dentistry, used for both functional restoration and cosmetic improvement.

At Dental on King, we use premium materials including porcelain and ceramic to create crowns that look indistinguishable from natural teeth. Our crowns are crafted to match the exact colour and translucency of your surrounding teeth.

Whether you need a crown after root canal treatment, to repair a broken tooth, or to improve the appearance of a discoloured tooth, we deliver beautiful, lasting results.

When Might You Need a Crown?

  • Cracked Teeth

    Protects and holds together a cracked tooth

  • Large Fillings

    Covers teeth with large fillings when little natural tooth remains

  • Root Canal

    Protects and strengthens teeth after root canal treatment

  • Worn Teeth

    Restores severely worn or eroded teeth

  • Cosmetic

    Covers discoloured or misshapen teeth

  • Bridge Support

    Anchors dental bridges to replace missing teeth

The Crown Process

1

Preparation

The tooth is reshaped to make room for the crown. Impressions are taken for the dental lab.

2

Temporary Crown

A temporary crown protects your tooth while the permanent crown is being made.

3

Fitting

The permanent crown is checked for fit and appearance, then cemented in place.

Frequently Asked Questions

How long do dental crowns last?

With proper care, dental crowns typically last 10-15 years or longer. Good oral hygiene and regular checkups help extend their lifespan.

Will my crown look natural?

Yes. We use high-quality porcelain matched to your natural tooth colour. Most people can't tell the difference between a crown and a natural tooth.

Is getting a crown painful?

The procedure is performed under local anaesthesia, so you won't feel pain during treatment. Any post-procedure sensitivity is usually mild and temporary.

How do I care for my crown?

Care for it like a natural tooth – brush twice daily, floss, and visit for regular checkups. Avoid biting very hard objects.

How many appointments are needed?

Typically two appointments – one to prepare the tooth and fit a temporary crown, and another 1-2 weeks later to fit the permanent crown.

Can a crown fall off?

While rare, crowns can come loose over time. If this happens, keep the crown and contact us immediately. Don't try to re-cement it yourself.
