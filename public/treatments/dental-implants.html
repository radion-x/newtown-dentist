Dental Implants

The gold standard for replacing missing teeth. Permanent, natural-looking solutions that restore your smile and confidence.

The Permanent Solution for Missing Teeth

Dental implants are widely considered the best option for replacing missing teeth. Unlike dentures or bridges, implants provide a permanent foundation for replacement teeth that look, feel, and function just like your natural teeth.

An implant consists of a titanium post that is surgically placed into your jawbone, where it fuses with the bone to create a sturdy anchor. Once healed, a custom-made crown is attached to complete your new tooth.

With proper care, dental implants can last a lifetime, making them an excellent long-term investment in your oral health and quality of life.

Why Choose Dental Implants?

  • Natural Look & Feel

    Implants are designed to look, feel, and function exactly like your natural teeth. No one will know the difference.

  • Permanent Solution

    Unlike dentures, implants are fixed in place. With proper care, they can last a lifetime.

  • Eat What You Love

    Enjoy all your favourite foods without worry. Implants restore full chewing function.

  • Preserve Jawbone

    Implants stimulate the jawbone, preventing the bone loss that occurs when teeth are missing.

  • Protect Other Teeth

    Unlike bridges, implants don't require grinding down adjacent healthy teeth for support.

  • Easy Care

    Care for implants just like natural teeth – brush, floss, and visit your dentist regularly.

The Dental Implant Process

What to expect during your implant journey

1

Consultation & Planning

We'll conduct a thorough examination, including X-rays and possibly 3D imaging, to assess your jawbone and plan the optimal placement for your implant. We'll discuss your options and create a personalised treatment plan.

2

Implant Placement

The titanium implant post is surgically placed into your jawbone. This is done under local anaesthesia and is typically less uncomfortable than a tooth extraction. You may have a temporary tooth during healing.

3

Healing & Integration

Over the next 3-6 months, the implant fuses with your jawbone in a process called osseointegration. This creates an incredibly strong foundation for your new tooth.

4

Crown Placement

Once healing is complete, we'll attach the abutment and your custom-made crown. The crown is designed to match your natural teeth perfectly in colour, shape, and size.

Implant Options

Solutions for different needs

Single Tooth Implant

Replace one missing tooth with a single implant and crown. The most common and straightforward implant treatment.

Implant Bridge

Replace multiple missing teeth with an implant-supported bridge. Typically requires two implants to support three or more connected crowns.

Implant-Supported Dentures

Secure your dentures with implants for a stable, comfortable fit. Choose from fixed or removable options.

Am I a Candidate for Dental Implants?

Most adults with missing teeth are good candidates for dental implants. The ideal candidate has:

  • ✓ Good general health
  • ✓ Sufficient jawbone density (or is suitable for bone grafting)
  • ✓ Healthy gums
  • ✓ Good oral hygiene habits
  • ✓ Commitment to follow-up care

Considerations

Certain conditions may require additional treatment or planning:

  • Bone loss in the jaw
  • Gum disease
  • Smoking
  • Diabetes (when well-controlled, implants are still possible)

Frequently Asked Questions

How long do dental implants last?

With proper care, dental implants can last a lifetime. The crown may need replacement after 10-15 years due to normal wear, but the implant itself can last indefinitely.

Is the implant procedure painful?

The procedure is performed under local anaesthesia, so you won't feel pain during placement. Most patients report that the discomfort afterward is less than they expected – often less than a tooth extraction.

How much do dental implants cost?

The cost varies depending on the complexity of your case, number of implants needed, and any additional procedures required. We'll provide a detailed quote during your consultation and discuss payment options.

How long does the process take?

The entire process typically takes 3-6 months, mostly for healing. The surgical procedures themselves are relatively quick. We can provide temporary teeth during the healing period.

Can implants fail?

Dental implants have a success rate of over 95%. Failures can occur but are rare, usually due to infection, poor bone quality, or certain medical conditions. Proper planning and aftercare minimise these risks.

Are there any alternatives to implants?

Alternatives include dental bridges (which require grinding adjacent teeth) and dentures (which are removable). We'll discuss all options to help you make the best decision for your situation.
