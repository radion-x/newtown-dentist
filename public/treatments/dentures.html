Dentures

Custom-fitted full and partial dentures to restore your smile, confidence, and ability to eat comfortably.

Restore Your Smile with Modern Dentures

Missing teeth can affect more than just your appearance – they impact your ability to eat, speak, and smile with confidence. Modern dentures offer a reliable, comfortable solution for replacing multiple missing teeth.

At Dental on King, we create custom-fitted dentures designed specifically for your mouth. Using high-quality materials and precise techniques, we ensure your dentures look natural, fit comfortably, and function well.

Whether you need full dentures to replace all teeth or partial dentures to fill gaps while preserving remaining teeth, we'll find the right solution for you.

Types of Dentures

Full Dentures

Replace all teeth in the upper or lower jaw. Custom-made to fit your gums and facial structure for a natural appearance.

Partial Dentures

Replace one or more missing teeth while preserving your remaining natural teeth. Clip securely to adjacent teeth.

Implant-Supported Dentures

Secured by dental implants for superior stability. No slipping or adhesives needed – the most secure option.

Frequently Asked Questions

How long does it take to get dentures?

The process typically takes several weeks, including impressions, try-ins, and adjustments to ensure perfect fit. Immediate dentures can be provided on the same day as extractions.

Will dentures feel natural?

There's an adjustment period as you get used to your new dentures. Most patients adapt within a few weeks and find their dentures comfortable and natural-feeling.

How do I care for my dentures?

Remove and clean dentures daily, brush your gums and tongue, and soak dentures overnight. We'll provide detailed care instructions.

How long do dentures last?

With proper care, dentures typically last 5-10 years. However, your mouth changes over time, so regular checkups ensure continued good fit.

Can I sleep with my dentures in?

We recommend removing dentures at night to give your gums a rest and prevent bacterial buildup. Soak them in water or denture solution overnight.

Will dentures affect my speech?

You may notice some changes initially, but most people adapt quickly. Practice speaking and reading aloud to speed up the adjustment period.
