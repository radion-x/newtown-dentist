Tooth Extractions

Safe, gentle tooth removal when a tooth cannot be saved. We make the process as comfortable as possible.

Gentle, Compassionate Care

While we always try to save natural teeth, sometimes extraction is the best option for your overall oral health. Whether due to severe decay, infection, crowding, or trauma, our team performs extractions with skill and compassion.

At Dental on King, we understand that the thought of having a tooth removed can be daunting. We take the time to explain the procedure, answer your questions, and ensure you're completely comfortable before, during, and after treatment.

Modern anaesthesia techniques mean the procedure itself is painless, and we'll provide comprehensive aftercare instructions to ensure smooth healing.

When Might You Need an Extraction?

  • Severe Decay

    When decay has damaged a tooth beyond repair

  • Infection

    Abscessed teeth that can't be treated with root canal

  • Crowding

    To create space for orthodontic treatment

  • Trauma

    Teeth damaged beyond repair by injury

  • Gum Disease

    Advanced periodontal disease affecting tooth stability

  • Wisdom Teeth

    Impacted or problematic wisdom teeth

What to Expect

1

Assessment

We'll examine the tooth and take X-rays to plan the best approach for removal.

2

Anaesthesia

Local anaesthetic ensures you won't feel pain during the procedure.

3

Extraction

The tooth is carefully removed with minimal impact to surrounding tissues.

4

Aftercare

We'll provide detailed instructions and may prescribe medication for comfort.

Frequently Asked Questions

Is tooth extraction painful?

The procedure is performed under local anaesthesia, so you won't feel pain during extraction. You may feel pressure, but no sharp pain. Post-operative discomfort is usually mild and manageable with over-the-counter pain relief.

How long does recovery take?

Most people recover within a few days to a week. You can usually return to normal activities the next day, though you should avoid strenuous exercise for 24-48 hours.

What should I eat after an extraction?

Stick to soft foods for the first few days â€“ soups, yoghurt, smoothies, mashed vegetables. Avoid hot foods and drinks for 24 hours and don't use a straw, as suction can dislodge the blood clot.

Should I replace the extracted tooth?

In most cases, yes. Missing teeth can cause remaining teeth to shift and affect your bite. We can discuss replacement options including implants, bridges, and dentures.

What is dry socket and how do I avoid it?

Dry socket occurs when the blood clot dislodges from the extraction site. Avoid smoking, spitting, using straws, and vigorous rinsing for 24-48 hours to prevent it.

Can I drive home after an extraction?

If only local anaesthesia is used, you can usually drive yourself home. However, if sedation is involved, you'll need someone to drive you and stay with you for a few hours.
