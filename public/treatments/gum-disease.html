Gum Disease Treatment

Protect your smile from the leading cause of adult tooth loss with expert periodontal care.

Understanding Gum Disease

Gum disease (periodontal disease) is an infection of the tissues that surround and support your teeth. It's caused by bacteria in plaque and is the leading cause of tooth loss in adults. The good news? It's preventable and, when caught early, treatable.

The early stage, called gingivitis, causes red, swollen gums that may bleed when brushing. Left untreated, it can progress to periodontitis, where the bone and tissue supporting teeth are destroyed.

At Dental on King, we take gum health seriously. We screen for gum disease at every checkup and provide effective treatment to stop its progression and restore gum health.

Treatment Options

Professional Cleaning

For early gingivitis, a thorough professional clean combined with improved home care is often sufficient to restore gum health.

Deep Cleaning (Scaling & Root Planing)

For more advanced cases, we clean below the gumline and smooth root surfaces to help gums reattach to teeth.

Ongoing Maintenance

After treatment, regular maintenance visits (often every 3-4 months) help keep gum disease under control.

Home Care Guidance

We provide personalised instruction on brushing, flossing, and other techniques to maintain healthy gums.

Frequently Asked Questions

Can gum disease be cured?

Gingivitis can be reversed with treatment and good home care. Advanced periodontitis can be controlled but not completely reversed. Early detection is key.

Does gum disease affect overall health?

Yes. Research links gum disease to heart disease, diabetes complications, and other health issues. Treating gum disease benefits your whole body.

Is gum disease treatment painful?

We use local anaesthesia to ensure you're comfortable during treatment. Most patients experience minimal discomfort afterward.

How can I prevent gum disease?

Brush twice daily, floss daily, avoid smoking, maintain a healthy diet, and attend regular dental checkups.
