Inlays & Onlays

Conservative tooth restorations that preserve more of your natural tooth structure.

The Middle Ground Between Fillings and Crowns

Inlays and onlays are custom-made restorations used to repair rear teeth with mild to moderate decay or damage. They offer a conservative alternative when a filling isn't sufficient but a full crown isn't necessary.

An inlay fits within the cusps (bumps) on the chewing surface of the tooth, while an onlay extends over one or more cusps. Both are fabricated in a dental laboratory from durable materials like porcelain or composite resin.

At Dental on King, we recommend inlays and onlays when appropriate because they preserve more healthy tooth structure than crowns while providing superior strength compared to direct fillings.

The Treatment Process

1

Preparation

Decay is removed and the tooth is shaped. Impressions are taken for the dental lab.

2

Temporary Filling

A temporary filling protects your tooth while your custom inlay/onlay is made.

3

Final Placement

The restoration is checked for fit and bite, then permanently bonded to your tooth.

Frequently Asked Questions

What's the difference between an inlay and onlay?

An inlay fits within the cusps of a tooth, while an onlay extends over one or more cusps. The choice depends on the extent and location of the damage.

How long do inlays and onlays last?

With proper care, inlays and onlays can last 15-30 years. They're more durable than direct fillings and help preserve tooth strength.

Are they noticeable?

No. Modern inlays and onlays are made from tooth-coloured materials that blend naturally with your teeth.

Is the procedure painful?

The procedure is performed under local anaesthesia, so you'll be comfortable throughout. Any sensitivity afterward is usually minimal.
