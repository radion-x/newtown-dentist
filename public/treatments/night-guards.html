Night Guards

Protect your teeth from grinding and clenching while you sleep.

Stop Teeth Grinding Damage

Bruxism – the grinding or clenching of teeth – affects many people, often during sleep when they're unaware of it. Over time, this can cause significant damage including worn teeth, cracks, jaw pain, headaches, and even tooth loss.

A custom night guard (occlusal splint) creates a protective barrier between your upper and lower teeth, preventing damage from grinding and reducing strain on your jaw muscles and joints.

At Dental on King, we create precisely fitted night guards that are comfortable to wear all night. Unlike bulky store-bought options, our custom splints won't disrupt your sleep while providing superior protection for your teeth.

Signs You May Need a Night Guard

  • Sleep Grinding

    Partner reports hearing grinding sounds at night

  • Worn Teeth

    Teeth appear flat, chipped, or worn down

  • Morning Headaches

    Waking with headaches or jaw pain

  • Jaw Tension

    Tight or tired jaw muscles upon waking

  • Tooth Sensitivity

    Increased sensitivity from enamel wear

  • Ear Pain

    Pain near the ear or TMJ clicking/popping

Frequently Asked Questions

Will a night guard stop me from grinding?

A night guard protects your teeth from the effects of grinding but doesn't stop the grinding itself. However, many patients find their grinding reduces over time with consistent use.

Is it comfortable to sleep with?

Custom night guards are much thinner and more comfortable than store-bought options. Most people adjust within a few nights and then find it comfortable.

How long do night guards last?

With proper care, a custom night guard typically lasts 3-5 years. Heavy grinders may need replacement sooner.

What causes teeth grinding?

Bruxism can be caused by stress, anxiety, sleep disorders, an abnormal bite, or certain medications. We can help identify contributing factors.

How do I clean my night guard?

Rinse with cool water after each use, brush gently with a toothbrush, and store in a ventilated case. Avoid hot water which can warp the material.

Can a night guard help with jaw pain?

Yes. By preventing clenching and reducing strain on jaw muscles, night guards often provide significant relief from TMJ-related jaw pain and headaches.
