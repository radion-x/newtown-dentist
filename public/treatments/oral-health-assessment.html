Oral Health Assessment

Comprehensive dental examinations to keep your smile healthy for life.

Your Foundation for Dental Health

A comprehensive oral health assessment is the cornerstone of preventive dental care. During this thorough examination, we evaluate every aspect of your oral health – from your teeth and gums to your bite, jaw joints, and soft tissues.

At Dental on King, we believe prevention is always better than cure. Regular assessments allow us to detect potential issues early, when treatment is simpler, more effective, and less costly.

Our examinations go beyond just looking for cavities. We screen for gum disease, oral cancer, signs of teeth grinding, and other conditions that could affect your oral and overall health. We also take time to understand your concerns and discuss any symptoms you may have noticed.

What We Check

  • Teeth

    Examining for decay, cracks, wear, and existing restoration integrity

  • Gums

    Checking gum health, pocket depths, and signs of periodontal disease

  • Soft Tissues

    Screening for oral cancer and examining tongue, cheeks, and palate

  • Bite & Jaw

    Assessing bite alignment and TMJ function

  • X-Rays

    When needed, X-rays reveal issues not visible to the eye

  • Risk Assessment

    Evaluating your personal risk factors for dental disease

Your Assessment Appointment

Frequently Asked Questions

How often should I have a dental checkup?

Most people benefit from checkups every 6 months. However, some may need more frequent visits based on their oral health status and risk factors.

Why are X-rays sometimes necessary?

X-rays reveal problems between teeth, under the gums, and in the jaw that can't be seen during a visual examination, allowing early detection.

What if I haven't been to a dentist in years?

Don't worry – we see many patients in this situation. We'll conduct a thorough examination and work with you to address any issues step by step.

Is a checkup included in the scale and clean?

Yes, our checkup appointments typically include both an examination and professional cleaning. Ask about our new patient special offer.

What should I tell my dentist during a checkup?

Share any symptoms you've noticed (sensitivity, pain, bleeding), changes in health or medications, and any concerns about your teeth or gums.

Do you screen for oral cancer?

Yes. Every comprehensive assessment includes a screening of soft tissues for any suspicious changes. Early detection is crucial for successful treatment.
