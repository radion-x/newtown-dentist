Oral Hygiene

Professional cleaning and personalised advice to keep your smile healthy and bright.

Professional Teeth Cleaning

Even with diligent brushing and flossing at home, plaque and tartar can build up in areas that are difficult to reach. Professional cleaning removes these deposits, helping to prevent tooth decay and gum disease.

At Dental on King, our thorough scale and clean appointments leave your teeth feeling fresh and smooth. We remove hardened tartar (calculus), stains from coffee, tea, and other sources, and polish your teeth for a brilliant finish.

Beyond cleaning, we provide personalised advice on improving your home oral hygiene routine. We'll demonstrate proper brushing and flossing techniques and recommend products suited to your specific needs.

What's Included in Your Clean

  • Polishing

  • Fluoride

Why Regular Cleaning Matters

Prevent Gum Disease

Tartar buildup irritates gums and can lead to gingivitis and periodontitis. Professional removal helps keep gums healthy.

Fresh Breath

Bacteria in plaque and tartar contribute to bad breath. Regular cleaning helps keep your breath fresh.

Brighter Smile

Professional polishing removes stains that make teeth look dull, restoring their natural brightness.

Early Detection

Regular visits allow us to spot developing problems before they become serious issues.

Frequently Asked Questions

How often should I have a professional clean?

Most people benefit from professional cleaning every 6 months. Those with gum disease or higher risk may need more frequent appointments.

Will the cleaning hurt?

Professional cleaning is generally comfortable. If you have sensitive teeth or gum issues, let us know and we can take extra care.

Can cleaning damage my teeth?

No. Professional cleaning by trained dental professionals is safe and won't damage your teeth or enamel.

Why do my gums bleed when cleaned?

Bleeding often indicates gum inflammation from plaque buildup. Regular cleaning and improved home care usually resolves this.

What's the difference between plaque and tartar?

Plaque is a soft, sticky film of bacteria that forms daily. When not removed, it hardens into tartar (calculus), which can only be removed professionally.

Will cleaning whiten my teeth?

Cleaning removes surface stains, making teeth appear brighter. For more dramatic whitening, ask about our professional whitening treatments.
