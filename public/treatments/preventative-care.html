Preventative Care

Prevention is the best medicine. Regular checkups and hygiene treatments keep your smile healthy for life.

Protecting Your Smile for Years to Come

At Dental on King, we believe that prevention is always better than cure. Our preventative care services are designed to catch potential problems early, maintain your oral health, and help you avoid more extensive (and expensive) treatments down the track.

Regular dental checkups and professional cleanings form the foundation of good oral health. Combined with proper at-home care, these visits can help prevent tooth decay, gum disease, and other dental issues before they develop into serious problems.

Our preventative approach doesn't just save your teeth – it can also save you money in the long run. Studies consistently show that investing in regular preventative care costs far less than treating dental problems once they've progressed.

Your Guide to Better Oral Health

Good oral hygiene starts at home. Here are our top tips for maintaining a healthy smile between dental visits:

1

Brush Twice Daily

Use a soft-bristled toothbrush and fluoride toothpaste. Brush for at least two minutes, covering all surfaces of your teeth. Don't forget your tongue!

2

Floss Every Day

Flossing removes plaque and food particles from between teeth where your toothbrush can't reach. It's essential for preventing gum disease.

3

Limit Sugar Intake

Sugar feeds the bacteria that cause tooth decay. Reduce sugary snacks and drinks, especially between meals.

4

Stay Hydrated

Drinking water helps wash away food particles and bacteria. It also helps maintain saliva production, which is your mouth's natural defence.

5

Visit Your Dentist Regularly

Even with excellent home care, professional checkups every 6 months are essential for catching problems early and maintaining optimal oral health.

Frequently Asked Questions

How often should I visit the dentist?

We recommend visiting for a checkup and clean every 6 months. However, some patients with specific conditions may benefit from more frequent visits. We'll advise you on the best schedule for your needs.

What happens during a checkup?

During your checkup, we'll examine your teeth, gums, and mouth for any signs of problems. We may take X-rays to see what's happening beneath the surface. Then our hygienist will perform a professional clean.

Is teeth cleaning painful?

Professional cleaning shouldn't be painful. You may feel some slight pressure or sensitivity in certain areas, but it should be comfortable. Let us know if you experience any discomfort and we can adjust our approach.

Can I claim my checkup on health insurance?

Yes! Preventative dental care is typically covered by most health insurance plans. We accept all major health funds and can process your claim on the spot with HICAPS.
