Root Canal Treatment

Pain-relieving treatment to save infected teeth. Modern root canal therapy is comfortable and highly successful.

Saving Teeth, Relieving Pain

Despite its reputation, root canal treatment is actually a pain-relieving procedure. When the soft tissue inside your tooth (the pulp) becomes infected or inflamed, it can cause severe toothache. Root canal therapy removes this damaged tissue, eliminates infection, and allows you to keep your natural tooth.

With modern techniques and anaesthesia, root canal treatment is no more uncomfortable than having a filling. Most patients are surprised at how easy and painless the procedure is.

At Dental on King, we use advanced technology to perform root canal treatment efficiently and comfortably, helping you get out of pain and back to your normal life.

The Root Canal Process

1

Diagnosis

X-rays and examination to assess the infection and plan treatment.

2

Anaesthesia

Local anaesthetic ensures complete numbness – you won't feel any pain.

3

Pulp Removal

The infected pulp tissue is carefully removed from the tooth's interior.

4

Cleaning & Shaping

Root canals are cleaned, shaped, and disinfected to remove all bacteria.

5

Filling

The canals are sealed with a biocompatible material to prevent reinfection.

6

Restoration

A crown is usually placed to protect and strengthen the treated tooth.

Signs You May Need Root Canal Treatment

  • Severe Toothache

    Persistent, throbbing pain that may radiate to jaw or ear

  • Temperature Sensitivity

    Lingering pain after hot or cold food and drinks

  • Swelling

    Swollen, tender gums near the affected tooth

  • Darkening

    Tooth has become darker or discoloured

  • Abscess

    Pimple-like bump on the gum near the tooth

  • Pain When Biting

    Discomfort when chewing or applying pressure

Frequently Asked Questions

Is root canal treatment painful?

No – root canal treatment relieves pain, it doesn't cause it. The procedure is performed under local anaesthesia and most patients find it no more uncomfortable than having a filling.

How long does the procedure take?

Most root canal treatments take 1-2 appointments of 60-90 minutes each, depending on the complexity of the case.

What's the alternative to root canal?

The only alternative is extraction. However, keeping your natural tooth is almost always the better option for your oral health and is often more cost-effective long-term.

How successful is root canal treatment?

Root canal treatment has a success rate of over 95%. With proper restoration and care, a treated tooth can last a lifetime.

Will I need a crown after treatment?

In most cases, yes. A crown protects the treated tooth and restores its full function. We'll discuss the best restoration option for your situation.

How do I know if I need a root canal?

Signs include severe toothache, prolonged sensitivity to hot or cold, darkening of the tooth, swelling, or a persistent pimple on the gum. A dental examination and X-ray will confirm.
