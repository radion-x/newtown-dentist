Frequently Asked Questions

Is teeth whitening safe?

Yes, professional teeth whitening is safe when performed under dental supervision. The products we use are clinically tested and we ensure your teeth and gums are healthy before beginning treatment.

Will whitening make my teeth sensitive?

Some patients experience temporary sensitivity during whitening, which typically subsides after treatment. We can recommend desensitising products if needed and adjust your treatment to minimise discomfort.

How white will my teeth get?

Results vary depending on the original shade of your teeth and the cause of discolouration. Most patients achieve several shades of improvement. We'll discuss realistic expectations during your consultation.

How long do results last?

With good oral hygiene and avoiding staining substances, results can last 1-3 years. Touch-up treatments can be done as needed using your existing custom trays.

Can everyone have their teeth whitened?

Most people are good candidates, but whitening may not be suitable for those with severe tooth decay, gum disease, or certain types of dental work. We'll assess your suitability during your consultation.

What causes tooth discolouration?

Common causes include coffee, tea, red wine, smoking, certain medications, and the natural ageing process. Professional whitening can address most types of external staining.
