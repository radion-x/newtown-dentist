Wisdom Teeth Removal

Expert assessment and gentle extraction of problematic wisdom teeth to protect your oral health.

When Wisdom Teeth Cause Problems

Wisdom teeth (third molars) typically emerge between ages 17-25, but many people's jaws don't have room for these extra teeth. When wisdom teeth can't fully emerge or grow at an angle, they become "impacted" and can cause pain, infection, and damage to neighbouring teeth.

Not all wisdom teeth need removal – some come through without issues. However, when they cause problems or are likely to in the future, extraction is often the best solution.

At Dental on King, we perform thorough assessments using X-rays to determine whether your wisdom teeth need attention, and we provide gentle, effective removal when necessary.

Signs Your Wisdom Teeth May Need Attention

  • Pain or Tenderness

    Aching at the back of your mouth or jaw

  • Swollen Gums

    Red, tender, or swollen gum tissue behind your back teeth

  • Bad Breath

    Persistent bad breath or unpleasant taste in your mouth

  • Difficulty Opening Mouth

    Stiffness or difficulty opening your jaw fully

  • Crowding

    Other teeth shifting or becoming crooked

  • Partial Eruption

    Tooth breaking through gum but not fully emerging

What to Expect

1

Assessment

We'll examine your mouth and take X-rays to assess the position of your wisdom teeth and plan the best approach.

2

Anaesthesia

Local anaesthetic ensures you're comfortable throughout. Sedation options are available for anxious patients.

3

Extraction

The tooth is carefully removed. For impacted teeth, a small incision may be made in the gum.

4

Recovery

We'll provide detailed aftercare instructions. Most people recover within a few days to a week.

Frequently Asked Questions

Does wisdom tooth removal hurt?

The procedure is performed under anaesthesia, so you won't feel pain during extraction. Post-operative discomfort is normal but manageable with pain medication and usually subsides within a few days.

How long is recovery?

Most people feel significantly better after 3-4 days and fully recover within 1-2 weeks. Following aftercare instructions helps ensure smooth healing.

Can I have all four wisdom teeth removed at once?

Yes, it's often more convenient to remove all problematic wisdom teeth in one appointment. We'll discuss what's best for your situation.

What can I eat after the procedure?

Stick to soft foods for the first few days – soups, smoothies, yoghurt, mashed vegetables. Avoid hot foods, straws, and anything hard or crunchy.

Do all wisdom teeth need to be removed?

No. If wisdom teeth have fully emerged, are healthy, positioned correctly, and can be cleaned properly, they may not need removal.

What age is best for wisdom tooth removal?

While there's no set age, removal is often easier in late teens to early twenties when roots aren't fully formed. However, we can safely remove wisdom teeth at any age.
